# Data Sources

AEGIS integrates multiple open-source conflict and political event datasets to ensure methodological rigor and cross-validation.

## 1. UCDP (Uppsala Conflict Data Program)

Purpose:
- Provides structured data on armed conflict onset and battle-related fatalities.

Why Included:
- Used to identify escalation onset dates.
- Academically recognized and widely cited.

Limitations:
- May undercount smaller-scale violence.
- Annual updates may lag real-time developments.

---

## 2. ACLED (Armed Conflict Location & Event Data)

Purpose:
- Event-level data on protests, battles, violence against civilians, and other conflict-related activity.

Why Included:
- Enables granular event analysis.
- Allows 30/60/90-day window modeling.

Limitations:
- Event reporting depends on media coverage.
- Potential geographic reporting bias.

---

## 3. Correlates of War (COW)

Purpose:
- Dataset on militarized interstate disputes and alliances.

Why Included:
- Supports identification of interstate tension escalation.
- Provides alliance and hostility-level context.

Limitations:
- Focused primarily on interstate actors.

---

## 4. ICEWS / GDELT

Purpose:
- Event-based political and diplomatic interaction data derived from news sources.

Why Included:
- Captures diplomatic rhetoric, sanctions, and political signaling.
- Useful for identifying early warning indicators.

Limitations:
- Dependent on news coverage density.
- Potential media bias in reporting tone and frequency.
